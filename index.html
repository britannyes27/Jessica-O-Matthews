Jessica O. Matthews

Jessica O. Matthews, born on February 13, 1988, is a Nigerian-American inventor, founder, CEO, and venture or capitalist. She is the cofounder of Uncharted, which was known to make Soccket (a soccer ball that can be used as a portable power generator). She went to Harvard College and she graduated from Harvard Business School. She was part of the “10 Most Powerful Women Entrepreneur” and as part of the “Most Promising Women Entrepreneurs”.

Achievements
- She began her career at the age of 19 through her invention of SOCCKET
- She has a degree in psychology and economics from Harvard University
- She has appeared on the covers of Forbes and Forbes Africa Magazines
- She founded an award-winning power infrastructure tech company at just 22 years old

- Jessica’s motto: “Succeed, not despite of who you are, but because of who you are, unapologetically.”
